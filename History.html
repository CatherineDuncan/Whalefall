Town
History

The town, originally named “Hammersmith”, was founded by Simon Knock. Before the lake that surrounds us today, the town spanned around the hill that now makes up our town. It was primarily full of German and French Immigrants

During an exploration mission into the forest, Simon Knock and a group of five hunters found the crumbling ruins of an old shrine. Its original use and organization affiliation is still unknown to this day.

With the town’s distance from other settlements, a group of four individuals banded together to found the Hammersmith News Letter. They received updates in letters from nearby towns, compiling them into their letter to advise the people of Hammersmith.

After a terrible storm, the basin of the town flooded, leaving the lower portion of the town in ruin. The population for the town was cut in half, and the remnants of the buildings were left to rot in the water.

The first bridge built leading from the island fell apart in the middle of the night on April 21st, 1860. The residents without boats were left stranded until the bridge was repaired six months later. No one knows the cause of the collapse.

The first railroad tracks were laid three miles from the town, causing a temporary population spike. The workers of the railroad frequented the establishments in town, such as the bars and inns. The people in town now can trace their families back to those workers, and they are honored in the city hall.

On December 16th, 1923, the Hammersmith Newsletter suddenly shut its doors. The team of five people were never seen or heard from again.

The first ever Lake Fargo parade runs through the town’s streets, celebrating the renaming of the town. Each business who wishes to participate can make a float for the parade to show their town spirit!

The Lake Fargo Diner opens its doors to the public! Its owners pride themselves on good, comforting food at all hours of the day. The current proprietors are the children of the original owners. A wonderful family business!

The First Presbyterian church, the oldest church in town, is forced to shut down. The congregation was too small, and therefore lost its funding over time.
